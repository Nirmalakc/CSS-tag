Chitwan National Park

Chitwan national park was established in1973 .It is located in chitwan district whch falls on terai region ,and granted the status of world heritage site in 1984. It covers a area of 952.63 sq km. It is Nepal's first national park. It is particularly rich in flora and fauna and home to one of the last populations of single horned rhinoceros and also is also one of the last refuges of the Bengal Tiger. This national park is extended over four districts chitwan, Nawalparasi, Parsa and Makwanpur. The park is surviving example of the natural eco systems of terai region and covers subtropical lowland ,wedged between two east and west river valleys as the base of the Siwalik range of outer Himalayas. The core area lies between Narayani and rapti rivers in the north and Nepal-india International boarder in the south ,over churia hills, and from the Dawney hills west of the narayani , and boarders with parsa wildlife to the east. In this national park more than 700 species of wild life ,113 species of fish and mugar crocodiles,68 mammal's species as well as 543 species of bird ,and 17 species of snakes have been recorded .

Shey Phokshundo National Park

Shey phokshundo national park is the largest national park of Nepal. It was established in 1984 to preserve a unique Trans- Himalayan eco system with a diversity of flora and fauna .The park's climatic differences, altitude variations, and different Zoo geographical regions support a diverse range of biotic systems. Shey phoksundo National park provides a diversity of spectacular landscapes. Elevations in the park range from 2130m in Ankhe to 6883m at the summit of kanjiroba Himal. Most of the park lies northof the Himalayan crest. Nepal's deepest and second largest lake, phoksundo lake lies in upper regions of suligad. It is situated in the Trans- Himalayan region of northwest Nepal. It covers an area of 3555 sq km in the districts of dolpa and mugu. Six reptiles 200 species of bird , 29 species of butterfly including habitat for endangered species like snow leopard , grey wolf, musk deer, and blue sheep as well as highest flying butterfly in the world, paralasa nepalicia are in this national park.

Rara National Park

Rara national park was established in 1976 with main objective of conserving high altitude biodiversity and endangered wildlife species in the majestic landscape of rara lake. It has ben enlisted in the Ramsar site in 2007 .Rara national park has been one of the popular domestic tourist destination since last five year after the opening of karnali highway. It has covered an area of 106 sq km in the mugu and jumla districts. It is the Nepal's smallest national park. Its main feature is rara lake at an altitude of 2990m .this park was established to protect the unique flora and fauna of the Humla and jumla region of Nepal .esspecially rara national park is pleasant during the summer only because it becomes extremely cold in winter due to high altitude it is situated . In this national park 51 species of mammal's, 241 species of birds,two species of reptiles and amphibians,and three species of fish have been recorded.
